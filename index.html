Dual Axis Solar Tracking System

Project Overview
The Dual Axis Solar Tracking System is a student project designed to maximize solar energy capture by automatically adjusting solar panels to follow the sun's movement throughout the day. This system improves energy efficiency compared to traditional static solar installations. Using sensors and control algorithms, the system tracks the sun's position across both horizontal and vertical axes, ensuring better panel orientation for improved energy generation from sunrise to sunset.

Key Features

  • Dual-Axis Tracking: Horizontal and vertical movement for complete sun tracking
  • Real-time Positioning: Advanced sensors continuously monitor sun position
  • Weather Protection: Automatic safety positioning during adverse weather
  • Manual Control: Basic monitoring and control capabilities
  • Energy Optimization: 20-35% increase in energy generation

With power factor monitoring capabilities and remote access features, this smart meter project helps monitor energy consumption, estimate bills, and provides remote machine control capabilities through a mobile interface.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive Monitoring: Measures current, voltage, and power consumption in real-time
  • Project Level: Designed for monitoring loads in educational and small-scale applications
  • Power Factor Analysis: Monitors power factor across transmission lines for efficiency
  • Global Access: Remote monitoring and control from anywhere in the world
  • Energy Analytics: kWh consumption tracking for accurate billing estimation
